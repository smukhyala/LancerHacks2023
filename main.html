What event marked the end of World War II?

The Battle of Stalingrad
The D-Day Invasion
The Surrender of Japan
The Fall of the Berlin Wall

Explanation: The end of World War II is typically marked by the unconditional surrender of the Empire of Japan, which occurred on September 2, 1945. The surrender took place aboard the USS Missouri, a United States battleship, and was accepted by General Douglas MacArthur, Supreme Commander of the Allied Powers, in the presence of representatives from several countries, including the United States, the United Kingdom, and the Soviet Union. This event marked the end of the world's largest and deadliest conflict and brought an end to the hostilities of World War II.
